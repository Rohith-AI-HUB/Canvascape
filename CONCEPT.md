# Canvascape Concept: The Spatial Workspace Browser

Canvascape is a revolutionary approach to browsing and digital organization, moving away from the linear tab-based systems of traditional browsers towards a **Spatial, Multi-Workspace Canvas**.

## 1. The Core Philosophy: Spatial Context
Most browsers treat information as a stack of hidden tabs. Canvascape treats information as **objects on a map**. 
- **Spatial Memory**: Humans are naturally better at remembering *where* something is rather than which index it occupies in a list. By placing websites, notes, and code on a 2D plane, you leverage your brain's spatial reasoning.
- **Visual Relationships**: You can group related websites together, link them with edges, and see the "big picture" of your research or project at a glance.

## 2. Hierarchical Organization: One Canvas, Many Worlds
The "One Canvas" architecture is divided into isolated **Workspaces** (e.g., Work, Research, Personal).
- **Independent Context**: Each workspace is its own "dimension" on the canvas. 
- **Workspace-Specific History**: When you close a tab in "Research," it stays in the research history. Switching workspaces gives you a completely fresh mental context.
- **Persistent Viewports**: The camera (zoom and pan) is remembered for every workspace. Switching to "Work" flies the camera exactly where you left off.

## 3. The Live IDE: Bridging Browsing and Creation
Canvascape isn't just for consuming; it's for building. 
- **In-Canvas Preview**: Create "Live IDE" cards where you can write HTML/CSS/JS and see the result instantly right next to your research tabs.
- **AI-Augmented Workflow**: AI cards on the canvas can "see" your open tabs and help you generate code or summarize information based on your current workspace's content.

## 4. Total Persistence
Everything in Canvascape is "sticky."
- **State Restoration**: If you close the app and reopen it, every window position, every zoom level, every theme toggle, and every AI conversation is exactly where you left it.
- **Zero Friction**: There is no "save" button; the workspace evolves with you in real-time.

## 5. UI/UX Principles
- **Minimalist "Glass" Design**: A dark, focused aesthetic that puts your content first.
- **Command-Centric**: Use the Command Palette or Composer to navigate and create at the speed of thought.
- **Fluid Motion**: High-performance animations (powered by Framer Motion) make navigating between workspaces feel like traveling through a digital universe.
